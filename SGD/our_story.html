Our Story

When Donna Barger graduated from Carlow College in the spring of 1981, Pittsburgh was experiencing a deep recession. The job market was literally non-existent, making it impossible for a newly graduated 21 year-old to land an interview much less find a position in her desired field of graphic design.

 

Freelancing was the only alternative. It was also excellent timing that Duquesne University had just launched its new Masters in Communications program. Enrolling into the program was an ideal opportunity to gain further education…while waiting out the economic challenges in the region.

 

With a successful freelance business and her parents’ encouragement, Donna founded Sewickley Graphics & Design in April 1983 and moved into a two-room, second floor space on 411 Beaver Street, Sewickley. Over the next 28 years, SG&D would relocate four more times to accommodate growth and change.

 

Fast-forward to June 2009, Donna made one of the most challenging decisions in her career - to relocate the company to the airport area and change its name to SG&D Communications & Design. It was the right move. Realigned and repositioned, SG&D is poised to take on the future.
